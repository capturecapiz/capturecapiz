    Brief Description

                President Roxas is a town located in the central part of Capiz, known for its agricultural economy centered on rice and coconut farming. The fertile lands surrounding the town help sustain its agricultural base, providing a livelihood for many of its residents. The town is also historically significant, being named after the country’s fifth president, Manuel Roxas. The scenic landscapes, including rivers and hills, add to the town’s charm, offering peaceful surroundings for eco-tourism.

                Culturally, President Roxas is proud of its heritage, and the town hosts various festivals that celebrate its traditions and local customs. The people of President Roxas maintain a strong sense of community, with a deep connection to their agricultural roots and historical significance. The combination of these factors makes President Roxas a noteworthy municipality in Capiz.
