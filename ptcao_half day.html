Capiz Short Tour:

A Journey Through History, Culture, and Nature

Discover the charm of Capiz with this short yet immersive tour, showcasing its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural wonders. Here’s a glimpse of the places you’ll explore:

Sites to be visit:

Ang Panublion Museum
A treasure trove of Capiz’s history and culture, this museum is housed in a historic water tank built in 1910. It features exhibits highlighting the region’s rich heritage, from pre-colonial artifacts to memorabilia of prominent Capizeños.


Birthplace of Pres. Manuel Roxas
Visit the ancestral house of President Manuel Roxas, the first President of the Republic of the Philippines. This site offers a glimpse into his life and legacy, with well-preserved artifacts and photographs documenting his contributions to the nation.


Sta. Monica Church and Panay Bell
Marvel at one of the oldest churches in the Philippines, built in 1774. The Sta. Monica Church is home to the iconic Panay Bell, the largest Catholic bell in Asia, cast from 70 sacks of coins donated by locals.


Pusô Making "(The "Puso" from Panay, Capiz)""
Experience the art of weaving pusô—traditional rice pouches made from coconut leaves. This centuries-old practice connects you to the culinary traditions and sustainable practices of the Panay people.


Banica Dried Fish Center
Explore the bustling Banica Dried Fish Center, a haven for seafood enthusiasts. This marketplace is a go-to destination for dried fish delicacies, reflecting the region’s vibrant fishing industry.


Capiz Tourist Information Center and Specialty Shops
This stop offers more than just information. Shop for unique Capiznon souvenirs, crafts, and local delicacies while learning more about the province’s tourism initiatives.


Spanggo Foods and Pasalubong Center
Delight in Capiz’s culinary offerings at Spanggo Foods, famous for its fusion of Spanish and Ilonggo cuisine. Don’t forget to grab pasalubong (souvenirs) like barquillos and piaya to take home a taste of Capiz.


Palina Greenbelt Ecopark
End your journey with a relaxing river cruise at Palina Greenbelt Ecopark. Witness the lush mangrove forests and serene waters while enjoying fresh seafood and the harmonious coexistence of nature and sustainable tourism.
