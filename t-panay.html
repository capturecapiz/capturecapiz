    Brief Description

                Panay, located in the southern region of Capiz, is an agricultural town that relies heavily on rice, coconut, and corn farming. The fertile soil and favorable climate make it a key player in the agricultural sector of Capiz. The town’s picturesque landscapes, with expansive fields and rolling hills, offer a serene environment for nature lovers and tourists. Panay also has historical significance, with landmarks that speak to its rich cultural past.

                The people of Panay are known for their deep connection to their traditions and their strong sense of community. Local festivals, which are celebrated throughout the year, showcase the town's cultural practices and agricultural heritage. Panay’s vibrant cultural life, combined with its agricultural wealth and natural beauty, makes it a noteworthy town in Capiz.
