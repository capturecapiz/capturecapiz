Province of Capiz

Capiz is composed of 16 municipalities and the component city of Roxas, its provincial capital. It is divided into two legistrative districts: Roxas City, Pa-ay, Panitan, Pontevedra, Maayon, President Roxas and Pilar comprise the first, and Ivisan, Sapian, Sigma, Dao, Mambusao, Dumalag, Jamindan, Dumarao, Tapaz and Cuartero, comprise the second. Roxas City and the municipalities of Panay, Pontevedra, President Roxas, Pilar, Ivisan amd Sapian are considered as coastal areas while the rest are landlocked.

The coast of Capiz suatains the vibrant fishing industry of the province. Its rich fishing grounds is the reason why Capiz is dubbed as the Center of the Seafood Trip Tourism in the Country.

Capiz is one of the six provinces in Western Visayas, located on the Northeastern portion of the island of Panay. It occupies the total land area of 2,633.2 square kilometers. Shaped like an open palm, it is situated at the heart of the Philippine archipelago at 11' 09 to 11' 40' North latitude and 122 11' to 123 05' East Longhitude. The Province is bounded by the Sibuyan Sea on the North, where Roxas City and the six coastal municipalities of Ivisan, Sapian, Panay, Pontevedra, President Roxas, and Pilar are largely dependent on fishing for their livelihood. It is bounded on the south and southeast by the Province of Iloilo Province, on the southwest bt the Province of Antique, and on the west and northeast by the Province of Aklan.
